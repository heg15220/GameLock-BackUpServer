Cut points were chosen by measuring each recording's energy envelope, not by
ear-free guesswork: the sources run 12-24 s of continuous material, and the owl
recordings are discrete calls separated by true silence, so an arbitrary window
would have played nothing at all. Each file now holds one gesture, with a fade
out and a re-run of loudness normalisation after the cut.

Note on graveyard-night-bed.mp3: BigSoundBank has no cemetery ambience as such.
This is a 24-second excerpt of a rural evening soundscape (wind and night
insects), which is what a graveyard bed is made of in practice. It is an
excerpt, not a seam-checked loop — see the game's audio module for how it is
looped at playback.

Path: src/games/sports/padel-arena/assets/sounds/

  File              Original                Ref     Edit
  ----------------- ----------------------- ------- --------------------------
  paddle.mp3        Tennis Ball: Bounce     s0584   1.64-1.94 s
  smash.mp3         Tennis Ball: Bounce     s0584   3.36-3.72 s
  floor.mp3         Tennis Ball: Bounce     s0584   10.48-10.78 s
  glass.mp3         Knock on a Glass Door#1 s0319   0.352-0.532 s
  clap.mp3          Applause #1             s2363   0.15-1.75 s
  whistle.mp3       Plastic Whistle #1      s1017   0.00-0.85 s

The three impacts come from one 37-second recording of a tennis ball bouncing
until it settles. Individual strikes were located with silence detection rather
than picked by eye — the impacts sit at 1.67 s, 3.38 s, 10.51 s and so on — and
each was cut with its own decay. They are the same ball at different energies,
which is what a paddle strike, a smash and a court bounce actually are.

glass.mp3 is one knock on a glass door. In padel the ball coming off the glass
wall is the sound that identifies the sport, and no oscillator gets near it. The
source is a knock-knock-knock — three strikes at 0.36 s, 0.54 s and 0.72 s — so
only the first is kept, cut at 0.18 s where its decay ends and before the second
begins. A single bounce has to sound like a single bounce.

clap.mp3 is "several people applauding indoors", chosen because it is applause
and nothing else. Recordings that mix in cheering, shouting or bar noise were
rejected: the crowd acknowledges the point, it does not celebrate it.

The net cue stays synthesized: a dead, muffled thud has no good CC0 recording,
and synthesis does that particular job well.
